Introduction

This page contains artifacts for the paper: “OVERTON” (submitted to ACM CCS 2024). The artifacts include OVERTON source code, VEINS & F2MD simulator version used for experiments, traffic scenario implementations, and generated data for the scenario grid we detailed in the paper.

  • OVERTON framework and instructions: GitHub
  • VEINS maps and scenarios: Repo
  • Generated traffic logs: Google Drive

VEINS Scenarios for Experiments

Attacker nodes are depicted in red and genuine nodes in green.

Manhattan Grid

This scenario implements a single-lane 10×10 grid map.

  • Single-lane roads with a 10×10 layout; intersections spaced 100 m apart.
  • 100 vehicles with varying attacker densities and a balanced mix of considered attacks.
Video: https://youtu.be/0aMBMmMEcPw

Erlangen Flock

This scenario simulates a realistic rush-hour with frequent congestion.

  • Modified Erlangen city map, single designated route (no detours).
  • 150 vehicles flock out of a parking lot every 6 s, causing occasional traffic jams.
Video: https://youtu.be/AvogtLSoI4g

IRTSX Map

This scenario emulates regular traffic in the IRTSX campus.

  • Small network → fast and predictable runs.
  • Traffic lights, pedestrians, crossroads; realistic path-loss models.
  • 300+ entities arbitrarily enter/leave the network.
Video: https://youtu.be/vBeQfSryIos

VEINS & F2MD Simulator

OVERTON works with both ITS-G5 (IEEE 802.11p) and C-V2X (3GPP PC5 Mode 4). We evaluate using VEINS & F2MD V2X traffic simulators. Installation instructions are available here.
